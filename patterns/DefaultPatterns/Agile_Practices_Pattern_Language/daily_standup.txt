DAILY STANDUP MEETING

I remember a team that worked in silos. Developers hid in their cubicles, and integration was a nightmare every two weeks. Then they started meeting for 15 minutes each morning, standing in a circle. Problems surfaced early, and the team began to self‑organize.

✥ ✥ ✥

A development team needs to synchronize daily, identify impediments, and align on goals. Long meetings kill productivity, and asynchronous communication can miss important context.

Therefore:
Hold a short, time‑boxed meeting every day where each team member answers three questions: What did I do yesterday? What will I do today? What is blocking me? Keep the meeting standing to encourage brevity.

✥ ✥ ✥

This practice is a cornerstone of Scrum and Agile. It works well with SPRINT PLANNING (sprint_planning.txt) to set daily goals aligned with the sprint. It also feeds into RETROSPECTIVE (retrospective.txt) where the team reflects on the standup’s effectiveness.

The standup reveals dependencies and impediments that can be tackled using OBSERVER PATTERN (observer_demo.txt) – team members become observers of each other’s work. It also encourages the kind of transparency needed for TEST‑DRIVEN DEVELOPMENT (test_driven_development.txt) to work well.
